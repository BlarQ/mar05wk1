Classes of Food

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's primary source of energy. They are made up of sugars, starches, and fibers.

Additionally, carbohydrates are essential for brain function and are involved in various metabolic processes. While they are an important part of a balanced diet, it's crucial to choose complex carbohydrates over simple ones to maintain stable blood sugar levels and overall health.

  1. Bread
  2. Rice
  3. Pasta
  4. Potatoes

Proteins

Proteins are essential for growth, repair, and maintenance of body tissues. They are made up of amino acids.

Proteins are the building blocks of life, playing critical roles in the growth, repair, and maintenance of body tissues.

  1. Chicken
  2. Eggs
  3. Beans
  4. Fish

Fats

Fats provide a concentrated source of energy and are necessary for the absorption of certain vitamins. They are composed of fatty acids.

Fats are essential macronutrients that play vital roles in the body, including providing energy, insulating organs, and assisting in the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

  1. Olive oil
  2. Avocado
  3. Butter
  4. Nuts

Vitamins

Vitamins are essential micronutrients that the body requires in small amounts to function properly. They play various roles in metabolism, immunity, and overall health.

There are two main types of vitamins: fat-soluble vitamins and water-soluble vitamins.

  1. Vitamin C
  2. Vitamin A
  3. Vitamin D
  4. Vitamin K

Minerals

Minerals are inorganic nutrients that are essential for various bodily functions, including bone health, fluid balance, and nerve transmission.

They are classified as macrominerals (needed in larger amounts) and trace minerals (needed in smaller amounts).

  1. Calcium
  2. Iron
  3. Potassium
  4. Magnesium

Water

Water is essential for life and makes up a significant portion of our body weight. It is involved in various physiological processes, including temperature regulation and waste removal.

Additionally, water-rich foods such as fruits and vegetables can contribute to overall hydration levels. Ensuring adequate water intake is crucial for maintaining overall health and well-being.

Tap water
Tap water serves as a readily accessible and commonly used source of drinking water in many households. It undergoes treatment processes such as filtration and chlorination to ensure safety and quality. It is versatile, used for drinking, cooking, and various hygiene purposes.
Mineral water
Mineral water is sourced from natural springs or wells, containing minerals such as calcium, magnesium, and sodium. Prized for its perceived health benefits and refreshing taste, it's often enjoyed as a hydrating beverage option.
Coconut water
Derived from young green coconuts, coconut water is a naturally refreshing and electrolyte-rich beverage. It contains essential minerals like potassium, magnesium, and calcium, making it effective for rehydrating after physical activity or dehydration.
Sparkling water
Also known as carbonated water or soda water, sparkling water is infused with carbon dioxide gas under pressure. It provides effervescence or bubbles and is available in various flavors. It's enjoyed as a refreshing alternative to sugary sodas or as a mixer in cocktails and mocktails.
